After observing how little access people have to medical treatment statistics, my colleagues and I decided to address the issue directly. findED.io is a webapp which combines hospital statistics from the Centers for Medicaid and Medicare Services Hospital Quality Initiative with data from Google Maps API to rank nearby hospitals by both total time until treatment and overall quality. For this project, my primary contributions were to the front end user experience, focusing on ways to quickly retrieve and convey information to the people who need it the most. Our site served over 50,000 unique visitors within 48 hours of launch.

FindED.io - A Patient Decision Tool for Determining the Optimal Emergency Room for Non-Ambulance Emergencies

Winner of the 2016 IISE Southeast Region Technical Paper Competition

Over one million pounds of high-purity zirconia are thrown away annually as a result of the dental crown milling process, producing needless waste. My senior design team formulated a viable recycling stream for this material with minimal overhead cost for all industry parties involved. Specifically, I helped with the materials characterization and established the final leg of the recycling supply chain between dental labs and manufacturing plants.

Project ReCap: Recycling of Dental Crown Byproducts

1st place Materials Science project at the 2016 Georgia Tech Capstone Expo

Bandit is a wearable with all the functionality of existing fitness trackers and then some. It houses six completely modular sensor bays large enough for anything from geiger counters to run-of-the-mill accelerometers. With a variety of mounting options à la GoPro, Bandit is the complete sensor package for anyone: surfers, rally drivers, triathletes, moms, nuclear physicists, etc. As team lead for this design project, my role consisted of configuring the electronics and making a working three sensor prototype capable of sensor hot-swapping.

Bandit - A Modular Sensor Package for Triathletes

Prosthesis technology is quickly catching up to meet the demands of everyday life. As part of the AMBER Lab, I am the project manager of AMPRO3, a transfemoral prosthesis featuring actuated knee and ankle joints. Utilizing a hybrid zero dynamics framework to drive the system toward human-inspired reference trajectories, AMPRO3 will serve as a platform for future cost of transportation and walking gait studies. In addition to integrating custom fabricated mechanical components, human-side transducers, and new software libraries, I have personally implemented four novel model-based adaptive and robust controllers alongside my graduate team.

Source for my custom API used to integrate an OptoForce six-axis load cell with the ankle joint for the measurement of ground reactions.

Examining the microstructure of printed components can yield fundamental relationships between printing pattern algorithms, laser energy density, and structural integrity. While working in the aerospace industry, I optimized the direct metal laser sintering algorithms used in the production of SpaceX's Inconel and Ti6Al4V combustion components. Traditionally impossible printing overhang angles are feasible with careful refinement, and this way, even completely spherical shells can be sintered without any interior support.

After observing how little access people have to medical treatment statistics, my friends and I decided to address the issue directly. findED.io is a webapp which combines hospital statistics from the Centers for Medicaid and Medicare Services Hospital Quality Initiative along with data from Google Maps and Directions API to rank nearby hospitals by both total time until treatment and overall quality. For this project, my primary contributions were to the front end user experience and data analysis, focusing on ways to quickly convey information to the people who need it the most.

Over 1 million pounds of high-purity zirconia are thrown away annually as a result of the dental crown milling process, producing needless waste. My senior design team established a viable recycling stream for this material with little to no overhead cost for all industry parties involved.

1st place Materials Science project at the 2016 Georgia Tech Capstone Expo

Bandit has all the functionality of existing fitness trackers and then some. It houses six completely modular sensor bays large enough for anything from geiger counters to run-of-the-mill accelerometers. With a variety of mounting options a la GoPro, Bandit is the complete sensor package for surfers, rally drivers, triathletes, moms, and nuclear physicists. As project manager, my role consisted of guiding concept development and producing a three sensor prototype capable of sensor hot-swapping.

As part of the AMBER Lab, most of my time is spent working with AMPRO3, a transfemoral prosthesis featuring actuated knee and ankle joints. Utilizing hybrid nonlinear controls to simulate human walking, AMPRO3 will serve as a platform for future cost of transportation and walking gait studies. Currently, I am developing methods for optical terrain analysis in order to facilitate online gait switching.

Source for my custom API used to integrate an OptoForce six-axis load cell with the ankle joint for the measurement of ground reactions.

In previous projects, I've optimized direct metal laser sintering techniques for increased component integrity. By examining the microstructure of printed components, relationships can be drawn between printing pattern algorithms and laser energy density. Doing so extends the capabilities of even the most advance printers, making previously impossible overhang angles feasible. With careful refinement, completely spherical shells can be sintered without any interior support.

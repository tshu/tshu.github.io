After observing how little access people have to medical treatment statistics, my friends and I decided to address the issue directly. findED.io is a webapp which combines hospital statistics from the Centers for Medicaid and Medicare Services Hospital Quality Initiative with data from Google Maps API to rank nearby hospitals by both total time until treatment and overall quality. For this project, my primary contributions were to the data analysis and front end user experience, focusing on ways to quickly retrieve and convey information to the people who need it the most. We served over 50,000 unique visitors within 48 hours of launch.

FindED.io - A Patient Decision Tool for Determining the Optimal Emergency Room for Non-Ambulance Emergencies

Over 1 million pounds of high-purity zirconia are thrown away annually as a result of the dental crown milling process, producing needless waste. My senior design team formulated a viable recycling stream for this material with minimal overhead cost for all industry parties involved. Specifically, I performed materials characterization and established the final leg of the recycling supply chain between dental labs and manufacturing plants.

Project ReCap: Recycling of Dental Crown Byproducts

Bandit is a wearable with all the functionality of existing fitness trackers and then some. It houses six completely modular sensor bays large enough for anything from geiger counters to run-of-the-mill accelerometers. With a variety of mounting options a la GoPro, Bandit is the complete sensor package for anyone: surfers, rally drivers, triathletes, moms, nuclear physicists, etc. As project manager, my role consisted of guiding concept development and actually making a working three sensor prototype capable of sensor hot-swapping.

Bandit - A Modular Sensor Package for Triathletes

Because there are 2,000,000 amputees in the US alone, developing intelligent limbs which can match and eventually exceed baseline functionality is a highly motivating goal. As part of the AMBER Lab, I am the project manager of AMPRO3: a transfemoral prosthesis featuring actuated knee and ankle joints. Utilizing nonlinear controllers to drive the system toward human-inspired reference trajectories, AMPRO3 will serve as a platform for future cost of transportation and walking gait studies. Current work revolves around experimentally validating novel robust and adaptive controllers on system hardware.

Robust Control of a Powered Transfemoral Prosthesis Device with Experimental Verification

Examining the microstructure of printed components can yield fundamental relationships between printing pattern algorithms, laser energy density, and structural integrity. In previous projects, I optimized the direct metal laser sintering techniques used in the production of SpaceX's combustion components. Traditionally impossible printing overhang angles are feasible with careful refinement, and this way, even completely spherical shells can be sintered without any interior support.

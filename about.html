A forcing function

Our minds change before we can consciously acknowledge the difference. Ideas reach the other side of the world in seconds, yet it's our physical existence that simultaneously bottlenecks and gives meaning to almost everything we do. I find myself most concerned with seeking ways to dismiss human shortcomings: those that we're born with and those that are imposed. How can we become faster, stronger, more informed, less distracted when confronted with "hard" limits? More importantly, I hope whatever answers there are increase our appreciation of the things we find beautiful.

About me

As a student of both Materials Science and Computer Science, I like to play in the mapping between idea space and reality. It's worthwhile to question how two very different things meet and interact, how disturbances in one affect the other. In the past, I've studied polymer crystallization kinetics, composite fiber additive manufacturing techniques, direct metal laser sintering, and healthcare systems optimization at Georgia Tech, Peking University, and SpaceX. My current research is on powered prostheses, which I find highly motivating due to their impact on helping people today and potential for human augmentation in the future.
